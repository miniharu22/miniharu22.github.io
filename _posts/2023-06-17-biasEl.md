## 2. Depletion Width

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/42.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In the case of the depletion region, we know that it is created by the **diffusion** of the carrier and the **recombination** of the carrier on the opposit side, leaving only the fixed charge.  

> * Forward bias  
> As a positive voltage is applied to the p-side and a negative voltage is applied to the n-side, the majority carrier of the Quasi-Neutral region are pushed out in the direction of the Junction by electrical repulsion, **reducing the width of depletion region**.    



> * Reverse bias  
> As a positive voltage is applied to the n-side and a negative boltage is applied to the p-side, the majority carriers are led to both ends of the Quasi-Neutral region by electrical attraction, **increasing the width of depletion region**.

## 4. Electric Field

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/44.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In the case of E-field, we know that it is caused by the bending of the Energy band in the depletion region, and the value is the same as the slope of the band bending.  

> * Forward bias    
> As the height of the potential barrier decreases, **the overall slope of the Energy band decreases.**  


> * Reverse bias  
> As the height of the potential barrier increases, **the overall slope of the Energy band increases.**
