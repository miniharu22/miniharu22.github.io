Band Diagram in Ohmic Contact, Ohmic Contact in Bias, Band Bending in Ohmic Contact 

## 1. Band Diagram in Ohmic Contact

Then, what if the metal's work function is smaller than silicon, contrary to the Schottky Contact?  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/10.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In this case, it can be seen that there are few potential barriers in both metal and silicon as shown in the above figure.  

As such, MS Junction, where there are few potential barriers, is called ***Ohmic Contact**.  

In addition, the depletion region is not formed in the Ohmic Contact, because electrons are always moving in the Junction part since Potential barrier does not exits.  

&nbsp;

## 2. Ohmic Contact in Bias

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/11.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In the case of Ohmic Contact, even if a forward or reverse bias is applied, there is no potential barrier for both, So only the direction of movement of electrons is differnt, the current can flow smoothly.  

Like ordinary metal, Ohmic contact has the property of allowing current to flow smoothly, it can operate as an **Interconnection** that transmits electrical signals (= current) between various devices.

## 3. Band Bending in Ohmic Contact 

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/12.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="Schottky Contact" alt=""/></p>

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/12.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="Ohmic Contact" alt=""/></p>

If we check the band diagram of Schottky contact and Ohmic contact when bias is applied, we can see that the entire band of silicon is bent only in Ohmic contact.  

This band's slope means that there is an E-field, and the existence of E-field means that a voltage is applied.  

But, Why is this only in Ohmic contact? This is due to the presence or absence of Depletion region.  

While explaining the PN Junction, we assumed that if there is a depletion region, all bias is applied only to that area. (**Depletion Approximation**)    

In Ohmic contact, since this depletion region does not exist, the bias is applied to the entire silicon. That's why silicon's energy band has a slope.
