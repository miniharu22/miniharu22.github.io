MS Junction, Metal, Work Function & Electron Affinity

## 1. MS Junction

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/1.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In the case of PN Junction that we have studied so far, if semicounductors made of the same material as siliocn are bonded, this time we will talk about **MS Junction** in which Metal and Silicon are bonded.  

There are usually two types of Junction referred to in semiconductors.

* Homo-Junction  
Same type of materials are bonded, such as PN Junction.

* Hetero-Junction  
Different types of materials are bonded, such as MS Junction.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/2.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

MS Junction can be used as two functions, one as **Rectifier** and the other as **Interconnection**, which allows current to flow smoothly like ordinary metals.  

These functions are operated by a mechanism called **Schottky Contact** and **Ohmic Contact**, so we will look into it.  

&nbsp;

## 2. Metal

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/3.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

First, we need to know about the characteristics of Metal before finding out MS Junction.  

Metal has a large number of free electrons and holes because $$E_F,E_C$$ and $$E_V$$ are almost overlapped. Because of these properties, Metal is sometimes compared to extremely doped semiconductors.  

In addition, if a excess carrier occurs in Metal, it is immediately recombined and extinguished because there are already so may holes and electrons. That is why in metal, the carrier's diffusion length & lifetime is zero.

## 3. Work Function & Electron Affinity

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/msjunction/4.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

There are some of the most important concepts in MS Junction.  

Looking at the figure above, unfamiliar symbol such as $$Φ$$ and $$χ$$ can be seen. These are symbols representing **Work Function** and **Electron Affinity**, and there meanings are as follows.  

* Work Function ($$Φ$$)  
- Minimal energy needed to elevate one electron to a Vaccum Level  
- $$Φ$$ = Vaccum Level - Fermi Level  

* Electron Affinity ($$χ$$)  
- Minimal energy needed to bring one electron located at $$E_C$$ to a Vaccum Level  
- $$χ$$ = Silicon's Work Function - ($$E_C-E_F$$)  

Here Vaccum Level is a hypothetical Energy Level arbitrarily set to model MS Junction and becomes a reference level for Work Functions and Electron Affinity.  

It should also be noted that in the case of Work Function and Electron Affinity, They are **invariant constant** no matter what external factors act because they are unique value of the material.
