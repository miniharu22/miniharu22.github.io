Accumulation, Depletion, Inversion with Low Frequency, Inversion with High Frequency, Ideal C-V Plot 

## 1. Accumulation

If the capacitance characteristics have been confirmed when DC voltage has been applied to the Gate of MOS so far, we will now check the capacitance according to the change of AC Small signal.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/53.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

First, when a small signal is applied in the Accumulation mode, the amount of charge formed on metal and silicon is as above.  

At this time, the white part is the charge generated by DC voltage, and the black part is the charge formed by AC Small signal.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/54.png" width="40%" height="40%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In accumulation mode, whatever bias is applied to the gate, $$\Delta Q_{acc}$$ corresponding to $$\Delta Q_m$$ formed in metal can be formed, indicating that the capacitance is determined by oxide.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/55.png" width="40%" height="40%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

Here, We must know that $$C_{ox}$$ = $$\frac{\epsilon_{ox}}{t_{ox}}$$.  

&nbsp;

## 2. Depletion

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/56.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In Depletion mdoe, when a small signal is applied to the Gate, a charge amount as much as $$\Delta Q_m$$ is added to metal, and the depletion width increases to form $$\Delta Q_{dep}$$.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/57.png" width="40%" height="40%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In accumulation mode, Only oxide exists between two charges, while in depletion mode, Depletion width is inserted along with Oxide, so it behaves as if $$C_{ox}$$ and $$C_{dep}$$ are connected in series.  

If this is induced mathematically, the entire capacitance can be described as follows.

## 3. Inversion with Low Frequency

&nbsp;

In Inversion mode, even bulk electrons are gathered on the surface.  

At this time, the tendency of capacitance is divided according to the frequency of AC.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/59.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

First, when $$Q_{inv}$$ changes by $$\Delta Q_m$$ with low frequency, the speed is slow, so the increase or decrease of the inversion charge can all be responded to.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/60.png" width="40%" height="40%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/61.png" width="40%" height="40%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

In other words, Only $$C_{ox}$$ exists because Surface, not Depletion width, acts as an electrode.  

&nbsp;

## 4. Inversion with High Frequency

&nbsp;

Conversely, if the frequency is high, there is not enough time to respond to changes in $$\Delta Q_m$$, making it impossible to respond to all of them with $$\Delta Q_{inv}$$.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/62.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

Inevitably, $$\Delta Q_{dep}$$ corresponds to $$\Delta Q_m$$.  

That is, it is expressed in series of $$C_{ox}$$ and $$C_{dep}$$ same as Depletion mode.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/MOSCAP/63.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

However, in Inversion mode, since the depletion width is already fixed to the maximum value ($$x_{dT}$$), the value of entire capacitance remains constant as the minimum value of capacitance in Depletion mode.  

&nbsp;
