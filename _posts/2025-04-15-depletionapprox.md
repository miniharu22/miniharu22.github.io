## 2. Cause of Electric field generation

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/13.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

Electric field is generated in the bent part of the energy band. The reasons are as follows.  

Each doped semiconductor is in the state of **Charge Neutral Density**. However, when different types of semiconductors are bonded, each majority carrier is combined with the majority carrier on the other side to **Recombination** due to **Diffusion** caused by the difference in the concentration of electrons and holes.  

After that, Only fixed charge by Dopant remains in the area where each carrier has escaped.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/14.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

The remaining Fixed charge by Donor and Acceptor creates Electric field, and this effect is expressed in the bending of the Energy Band Diagram.  

Electric field generated in this way is call ***Built-in E-field** because it is not an external action, but an inherent occurrence in the semiconductor.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/15.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

**Drift** generated by electric field formed in this way acts in the opposite direction of carrier diffusion, which soon serves to prevent diffusion of the carrier. When the Drift and Diffusion are offset, each majority carrier is in place.  

However, from a different perspective, It is okay to understand it as the role of ***Potential Barrier** that prevents the carrier from moving over to higher energy band.

## 3. Depletion Region (Space Charge Region)

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/16.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

Then, How will the charge densitu in the semiconductor appear after the PN Junction?  

Area where the carrier is not leaked maintains charge neutrality as it is, but on the contrary, the area where only fixed charge remains is not electrical neutral. These areas are called **Depletion Region** and the charge denisity of each area can be expressed as the amount of charge equivalent to the corresponding doping concentration as above.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/17.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

So why is it named 'Depletion'?  

Calculating the concentration of the mobile carrier in each area, the concentrations of the majority carrier in n-side and p-side are the same as those of the dopant concentration, respectively, but only the fixed charge remains in the junction where the carrier is leaked by Diffusion.  

That's why it's called **Depletion region**, which means that Mobile carrier is a non-existent area.   

&nbsp;

## 4. Depletion Approximation

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/18.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

However, it does not make real sense that the concentration of the carrier in the semiconductor suddenly becomes zero based on a specific point.  

In fact, only the carrier concentration has decreased exponentially in the depletion region and the carrier itself exists, but in this case, the calculation of device characteristics becomes complicated.  

So to solve this problem, we assume that there is no mobile carrier in the depletion region. This assumption is referred to as **Depletion Approximation**.
