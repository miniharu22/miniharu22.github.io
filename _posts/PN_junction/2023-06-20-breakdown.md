Breakdown, Zener Effect with Tunneling, Avalanche Effect with Impact Ionization, Impact Ionization with E-field

## 1. Breakdown

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/56.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

According to the above I-V Plot of PN Diode, the current increases exponentially in the Forward bias, but the current is limited by the potential barrier in the Reverse bias.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/57.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

However, in reality, when the reverse bias rises above a certain reference value, the current flowing through the device is suddenly amplified.  

This phenomenon in which the current is not limited and suddenly amplified is called **Breakdown**.  

There are two mechanisms of this Breakdown : **Zener Effect & Avalanche Effect**  

&nbsp;

## 2. Zener Effect with Tunneling

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/58.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

**Zener Effect** is basically a mechanism that occurs in very highly doped  semiconductors.  

In this case, in the Equilibrium state, Fermi Level would be very close to $$E_C$$ and $$E_V$$ as above. If the Reverse Bias is applied very strongly on this device, the Energy Band diagram would change as shown below.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/59.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

Then the Constant Fermi Level will differentiate into a Quasi-Fermi Level very colse to $$E_C,E_V$$, and as the Potential Barrier becomes very high, the bending of Energy Band also increases rapidly.  

At this time, from the point of view of electrons located at $$E_V$$ on the p-side, the Bandgap will be very narrow. In this situation, the probability of electrons passing through the potential barrier in quantum mechanics also increases.  

In addition, if the electrons pass through the narrowed potential barrier, it moves to $$E_C$$ on the other side and acts as **Free electron**, resulting in Breakdown as current flows.  

This phenomenon of electrons passing through the potential barrier quantum mechanical is called **Tunneling**, and the mechanism by which current is generated is called **Zener Breakdown**.  

&nbsp;

## 3. Avalanche Effect with Impact Ionization

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/60.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

Unlike the Zener Effect, Avalanche Effect can occur even if the doping concentration is not so high.  

The mechanism of Avalanche Effect is as follows.  

1. With Potentail Barrier very high due to Reverse Bias, electrons on the p-side are shifted to the other side by positive voltage applied to the n-side.

2. At this time, the Potential energy possessed by the electron is replaced by Kinetic energy by the conservation of energy.
   
3. This Kinetic Energy generates e-h pair.
   
4. Electrons generated in this way move back, generating kinetic energy and creating electrons in a chain.  

This phenomenon in which electrons are exploded in a chain reaction is called **Impact Ionization**. And the mechanism by which the current is generated by this phenomenon is **Avalanche Effect**.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/61.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

The principle by which the e-h pair is created in Impact Ionization is as follows.  

Kinetic Energy replaced by Potential Energy is affected by the E-field of Depletion region.  

As the strength of E-field is amplified due to Reverse Bias, electrons gain high acceleration, and the accelerated electrons collide with atomic nucleus. If the kinetic energy of the collided electron is greater than the combined energy of nucleus & electrons, e-h pair is created.  

&nbsp;

## 4. Impact Ionization with E-field

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/62.png" width="50%" height="50%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

As mentioned earlier, in order for Impact Ionization, the kinetic energy of electrons must be greater than or equal to the binding energy of atomic nucleus, that is, Bandgap Energy($$E_G$$).  

To the end, E-field must be generated above a certain reference value to accelerate electrons.  

&nbsp;

<p align="center"><img src="/assets/images/pnjunction/63.png" width="70%" height="70%"  title="" alt=""/></p>

While Studying Built-in Electronics, We derived a formula to find the maximum value of E-field in the depletion region. If this value is greater than the strength of E-field required for Impact Ionization, Avalanche Breakdown occurs.  

In general, the value of Impact Ionization E-field ($$E_{critical}$$) required in Silicon semiconductors is $$10^6 [V/cm]$$.
